Methacholine is a bronchoconstrictor, meaning that it causes the airways in the lungs to close up. The Methacholine Challenge is a test used to measure hyperreactivity: how easy is it for the airways to close? To complete the test, a doctor will give a patient different doses of methacholine, starting at a low dose and increasing up to 2 mg. After each dose, the doctor will measure the FEV1 score to test the lung function.
y: (Final FEV1 Score - Initial FEV1 Score)/ Initial FEV1 Score
x: Final cumulative dose of methacholine (add all the doses)
O'Connor Slope = y / x
